Breeding

1. Use High Quality Parent Flowerhorns


If you want high quality results, it is logical that you should use the best materials you can find. Do not fall into the trap of breeding low quality flowerhorns expecting that high quality fry will come out of it. If you want to breed for fun, you may use average quality breeders. However, if you are really serious in producing high quality flowerhorns, invest in high quality breeders.

2. Properly Prepare Your Flowerhorn Breeders


This flowerhorn breeding tip significantly improves hatching rate. Be sure to allow at least 15 days before you introduce your breeders to each other. During that time, feed them frequently in small portions. After the breeding program, allow your flowerhorns some time off before you breed them again.

3. Use the Appropriate Tank Size for Breeding


Flowerhorns become more aggressive when mating. To reduce the risk of injury, provide them enough room. I recommend using a 50-gallon tank as a minimum requirement. If you have a 4-feet tank (around 70 gallons in capacity), use it. More tips of tank sizes.

4. Properly Cull Your Fry


Most inexperienced flowerhorn breeders are puzzled how successful ones manage to produce only high quality output. The secret may just be that they do not show you the undesirable ones. For an average fry population of 1,000, I would say that only around 50 (5%) of those are good quality. When the fry community is two months old, I normally would have culled half of them already. Before they reach 3 months old, you could no longer see any low quality flowerhorn in my tanks.

5. Never Give Up


It is seldom that a newbie breeder succeeds on his first try. If you think you have done everything to ensure success, it would not be long before you are rewarded. It could be just that your breeders were not yet ready. Just keep on going. Flowerhorn breeding is not an innate talent nor is it mystical; it is a skill developed through persistence. Incidentally, it is also a very fulfilling activity for hardcore hobbyists. Good luck!

Nitrogen Cycle

• In a nutshell it can be said that the nitrogen cycle in aquariums is the process of harmful toxins being broken down by nitrifying bacteria to reduce the risk to your fish.
• The first phase of the nitrogen cycle starts on day 1 and typically lasts for 1 to 2 weeks. In this phase, the water becomes cloudy due to a bacteria bloom and ammonia starts building up in the tank. Small water changes performed on a daily basis control this bacteria bloom and prevent the tank water from being overloaded with the harmful toxin ammonia.
• The second phase of the nitrogen cycle starts after the first phase has completed and typically lasts between 2 to 3 weeks. During this phase ammonia from fish wastes and left over food is broken down to nitrites. Small water changes done thrice a week ensure that the bacteria present in the tank can do their job and the nitrite levels don’t reach a toxic stage.
• The third phase of the nitrogen cycle starts when the second phase has been completed and lasts for 2 to 3 weeks. During this phase nitrites are broken down to nitrates. Nitrates are considered to be the least harmful of the 3 toxins that are the by-product of new water in the home aquarium. Small water changes done twice a week for the entire tenure of keeping an aquarium, ensure that the nitrate levels stay below the toxic level. An ammonia testing kit along with nitrite and nitrate testing kit can be used to ensure that the toxin levels are kept within an acceptable limit.

Types of Nitrogen Cycles

• Fish Cycle: The fish cycle consist of using fish that are hardy and can adapt to a variety of conditions. A small shoal of Barbs or a single carp might be used to complete this cycle. Small water changes and a controlled diet with little to none left over food are the keys to a successful fish cycle. Once your aquarium has completed the 3rd phase of the aquarium cycle, you can replace the hardy fish with your selected species of exotic fish. The fish cycle can take anywhere from 4 to 6 weeks to complete.
• Fishless Cycle: The fishless cycle is best completed with nitrifying bacteria that are available in bottles by aquarium product manufacturers. The instructions on the bottle are clear and indicative thus they must be followed to the last letter. The highlights of using the fishless cycle is the quick turnaround time of 1 to 2 weeks. This type fo aquarium cycle is ideal for hobbyists who do not want to stress out their fish and prevent death due to initial mistakes that can happen during the learning and experimenting phase.

Temperature


• Cold water fish usually do not need a heater, the only exception is if your home is situated in a place that experiences freezing temperatures regularly.
• Most exotic fish that are tropical in nature thrive at a temperature of 26 to 28 degrees Celsius/ 78 to 82 degrees Fahrenheit. The exact temperature will depend on the species that you want to keep, for instance flowerhorns thrive at 28 degrees C/82 degrees F when healthy.
• To ensure that the water temperature is regulated at all times, it is crucial to use a heater from a good brand with an inbuilt thermostat. The thermostat will cut off the temperature when it reaches the desired mark and will reheat the tank again when the temperature falls by 1 or 2 degrees.
• It is important to keep the heater switched on during the day and even at nights especially during winter, monsoons and fall seasons.

PH For Your Exotic Fish


• To ensure that exotic fish thrive in a home aquarium, it is important to regulate the PH of your aquarium. This can be done by either using natural or artificial PH buffers. Natural PH buffers are safer to use and can be left in the tank for a few months, after which they need to be replaced. Artificial PH buffers are sold in the form of liquids that are primarily made from chemicals and need to be used with extreme care.
• Driftwood and almond leaves are used to get an acidic PH. Both these organic compounds release tannins in the tank water that reduce the PH of the aquarium over a period of time. Crushed coral and crushed sea shells are used to get an alkaline PH. Both these organic compounds harden the water. Use the selected organic compounds in limited quantities and test the PH of the water with a PH kit to make sure that your tank has a suitable PH. As a general rule, the PH of the tank water should not be below 5 or above 8.5.
• While using either driftwood or crushed corals, make sure that the items you use are devoid of sharp edges as they can cause severe damage to the delicate structures of exotic fish. As a general rule, bog wood should not be used in aquariums as bog wood will decay over time which will deteriorate the quality of your water. This will in turn cause a spike in ammonia which can be fatal to your exotic fish.
• Before using natural and organic compounds as PH buffers, ensure that you wash the selected items with plain warm water, even boil driftwood if needed. As far as possible, do not collect these items from the beach or woods on your own as you do not know the origin of these items and the chemicals they have been exposed to before you collected them.
Final Words Other than following the scientific principles mentioned above, remember never to expose your exotic fish to unnecessary chemicals. Soap and house hold cleaners are toxic to exotic fish and can cause fatalities. While using a sponge, bucket and other tools to clean your aquarium, always ensure that you use dedicated equipment for your fish and do not allow these equipment to come into close proximity of soaps and household cleaners.

selecting the right flowerhorn

everybody here wants a MP but don't want to spend on it , I have seen people buying local breeds or simply cheap quality fhs and asking will it develop monster kok or y it doesn't have colours like other MP flowerhorns. Answer is SIMPLE it is always good to spend extra pennies for good quality flowerhorn then later regretting .. Conclusion:- if u want MP then spend accordingly .don't expect a 1$(60rs) flowerhorn to turn into 100$ one ..its no magic. expensive fhs have good genes and quality doesn't come cheap ..

don't spend everything on flowerhorns

many new hobbyist purchase costly flowerhorns but fail to groom them due to poor equipment they got..it is equally necessary to have good equipments . heater ,filter is must to provide him good filtration with stable temperature ..anything below 26°c can cause health issues to ur fh ..it is recommended to keep temperature between 28°-30°c ..also there should be good filtration system to keep tank clean .

weekly water changes

dont just throw fh in tank ,feed him twice thrice and expect him to become MP, water quality also matters it is important to do water changes weekly to provide him better water quality and remove nitrates from water..better water quality equals to better health and ultimately results in vibrant flowerhorns.always use dechlorine to remove chlorine from water .

food

always feed ur flowerhorns quality stuff. Don't spend unnecessary amount of money of costly food .there are many pocket friendly food available in markets from XO,OKIKO,WILD,HIKARI ETC. Young frys or juvi should be feed food with minimum 45% protein for speedy growth and better body formation and for full grown adults feed them staple food which do not contain extreme colourants and fats. Also never overfeed ur fhs just because u feed him too much he won't grow all in one month but will have negative impact on him ..

tank size

tank size plays and important role for ur flowerhorns. Fh is a big cichlid and needs big tank for proper growth of head,colour,body . anything less then 2feet is a complete NO for making it flowerhorns reach there max potential ..a minimum of 50gallon(3 feet) is recommended for complete growth of flowerhorn. Smaller tank also makes growth of ur beloved flowerhorns get stunned and do not show there exact colours and kok..

no sharp objects or gravels

don't put sharp objects in ur tank for the sake of it eyes. For u it seems beautiful but fh is aggressive and will tend to decorate his space according to him ..there might be chances of him getting hurt also gravels should not be sharp as flowerhorns are active diggers , a small cut from gravels can create infection in ur fh..
In short following is required for a health flowerhorn in ur tank
1)tank (bigger the better)

2)weekly water changes (healthy environment)

3)stable temperature (stable non fluctuating conditions)

4)good quality food(for keeping internal organs intact and also increase flowerhorns life)
